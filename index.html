Among Intelligences

An interactive Romanian-language SF book inspired by NASA's Artemis 2 mission. By Alexio.

The Premise

On day three of Artemis 2's lunar flyby, a hydraulic rupture forces the four-person crew — Wiseman, Glover, Koch, Hansen — to choose between ballistic re-entry and an emergency lunar landing. Houston offers three landing zones. The book explores all of them. And more.

17 Survival Scenarios

Each landing site (south pole, equator, 45°N) is split into four sub-scenarios based on lunar position relative to Earth and sunlight. Plus five extreme scenarios in deep space, including a Carrington-event solar storm and a failed rescue. The crew survives all 17 — but the survival is not the story.

Three Layers of Intelligence

The book builds toward something larger than a survival story. What begins as a procedural thriller — four astronauts, dwindling oxygen, a moon that wasn't expecting them — reveals itself, layer by layer, as a meditation on intelligence itself: who tells a story, who simulates reality, and where the boundary between the two disappears. The title is not a description of the setting. It is a position description for the reader.

108 Reviews

Beyond the story: 108 fictional reviews from literary critics, philosophers, readers, future AIs, alien intelligences, stars, hyper-civilizations, and entities from the end of the universe. Each rates the book on its own scale — from black holes that find it irrelevant to dark matter that recognizes itself in the unseen narrator.

Why It Matters

This is one of the first long-form fictional texts that uses AI as both subject and co-author transparently. Romanian SF reaches back to Ion Hobana and Vladimir Colin; this book pushes the tradition into the post-human era. It's free, runs entirely in your browser (single HTML file, offline-capable), and respects your privacy by design — no tracking, no cookies, no analytics.
